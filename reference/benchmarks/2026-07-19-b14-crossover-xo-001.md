Derived groupings:

- The first occurrence of each action in report items 1–7 took `701,662.5 ms` (`701.66 s`, 11.69 minutes), or 35.424% of the total.
- Repeated report items 8–9 took `1,279,112.0 ms` (`1,279.11 s`, 21.32 minutes), or 64.576% of the total.
- The three largest individual entries—the repeated bounded-support action, 2D chair layout/fit, and repeated 3D preparation—accounted for 73.918% of total wall time.
- The repeated bounded-support action was 6.604 times the wall time of its first occurrence.
- The second 3D preparation was 1.063% slower than the first; this report therefore demonstrates no warm-reuse saving for that action.
- Total CPU time divided by wall time is 99.552%, consistent with approximately one fully occupied CPU core overall. This does not prove that every internal operation is single-threaded.
- The session summary implies approximately 2,351.3 MB RSS and 80 document objects at the measurement start, ending at 2,556.6 MB and 89 objects.

## Initial findings, not yet root causes

1. **Repeated bounded support is the first investigation target.** Its 6.604× increase may indicate changed inputs, invalidated caches, document-state growth, repeated exact-shape construction, or a different internal path. The report alone cannot choose between them.
2. **Chair analysis, support, 2D fit, and 3D preparation dominate the first pass.** Together they make the architecture's lightweight-editing and deferred-exact-geometry seams directly relevant, subject to result equivalence.
3. **The repeated 3D action shows no useful reuse.** It may intentionally rebuild exact geometry, but that contract and cleanup behaviour need to be characterised before optimisation.
4. **Memory cannot be explained by object count alone.** Geometry commit reports `+154.3 MB` while reducing the document by seven objects. Retained shapes, tessellation/display data, caches, recomputes, or allocator behaviour require measurement.
5. **The starting process was already large.** An inferred 2.35 GB starting RSS makes fresh-process cold runs essential.

These are hypotheses for profiling and characterisation, not permission to change calculations, skip geometry, or weaken validation.

## Reproducibility status

Known from the operator clarification:

- FreeCAD was not freshly started.
- Whole-workflow timings were not reset immediately before item 1.
- The work used a long-lived existing document rather than a new controlled document.
- No parameter changes were reported between the repeated late-stage entries.

Still needed to make the case reproducible:

- What curve/easement, track-spacing, host, turnout/crossover, timber, chair, support, and display/export settings produced `XO-001`?
- What exact actions occurred between items 7, 8, and 9, and why were those stages recorded more than once?
- Were items 8 and 9 intentional warm/reuse checks, retries, or a response to another edit?
- Did every item run against the same target identity and output scope?
- Did this run use the Phase 0 FreeCAD 1.1.1 Flatpak environment?
- What report results from the same recipe after a fresh-process restart?

Once the recipe is fixed, capture at least three cold runs and three unchanged-result reuse runs where the workflow supports reuse. Do not average the repeated action in this report with its first occurrence unless their starting states and intent are proven equivalent.

## Instrumentation boundary

The B14 report label says “Whole workflow”, but current timers cover the guided turnout/crossover path. Curve/easement creation, station and multiple-track preparation, general editing, explicit validation, and every production-export boundary do not yet form one reconciled product-pipeline report.

Phase 1 will inventory those gaps and add common instrumentation before the affected capabilities are optimised or migrated. The instrumentation must distinguish nested versus sequential stages, cold versus warm state, edit-only versus edit-through-export cost, and must reconcile totals without changing product behaviour.
